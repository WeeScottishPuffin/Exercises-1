Orientation internship


    During the three days of my orientation internship, I was given simple exercise to determine my skills. Those exercises weren't that difficult due to my preexisting programming skills. Later on, I decided to redo some exercises to see if I have improved.

    Day 1

  • Exercise 1: An old aboutme page.

    • I was given an image to recreate in html/css/javascript and no further restrictions. This was my first exercise and also by far the easiest.

  • Exercise 3: A non-responsive (and non-resizable) recreation of a webpage.

    • I was again, given an image to recreate. This exercise was still easy, but a slight step up in complexity from Exercise 1. They didn't require resizability or responsiveness.

    Day 2

  • Exercise 5: A simple password generator. (redux)

    • During this exercise, I was given both an image and some requirements. It took a bit more time than either of the first 4 exercises, but I was able to complete it with easy. I added a few functions that weren't originally required; a copy button and the 'animation' of the text.

    Day 3

  • Exercise 6: An elevator panel

    • I was given some requirements and free reign to create an elevator panel in html/css/javascript. It was still quite easy, but certainly more complicated than any of the previous exercises. I also added some music and sound effects, both of which weren't required.

  • Exercise 7: An alarm webapp.

    • I was required to make a simple alarm system in html/css/javascript. I made this on the last day of my internship, and hadn't the time to really polish it. I still managed to create a working product without too much of a challenge.
